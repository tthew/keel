## Notes

- **iter-266 NOVEL LESSON — container-loopback ≠ host-loopback under Docker userland-proxy (sshd_config `ListenAddress 127.0.0.1` pitfall).** Story 2.12's original draft contained a "defence-in-depth" claim pairing a host-side `127.0.0.1:2222:2222` publish with a container-side `ListenAddress 127.0.0.1` directive. Subagent 4's attack-surface review surfaced this as CRITICAL: Docker's published-port forwarding lands inbound packets on the container's eth0 (iptables DNAT under userland-proxy=false, or docker-proxy re-origination under userland-proxy=true default) — NOT on the container's 127.0.0.1. Sshd bound to container-loopback would SILENTLY reject ALL inbound traffic under both modes; AC 3 ("sshd accepts connections from host-127.0.0.1") would be broken at the one path that should work. Fix: remove `ListenAddress 127.0.0.1`, rely solely on host-side published-port confinement. Generalisation for future-Ralph when reviewing container-runtime security claims: container-loopback is ISOLATED from host-loopback; inbound from the host arrives on eth0 regardless of the host-side bind address. The "defence-in-depth" instinct that couples host-side + container-side loopback binding is a pattern TRAP — the two `127.0.0.1`s are not the same interface. Applies to every future story that publishes a port with operator-facing protocol (Story 2.13 healthcheck, future Epic 4 service proxies, Epic 6 pg-init RLS debugger port).
